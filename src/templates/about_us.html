We came up with this idea in our machine vision course at the University of Rochester. Several of our friends were ASL majors and minors, and made us aware of how extensive and vibrant the deaf community was in Rochester. We believed this project could be an excellent demonstration of socially responsible technology. We sought to build an vision system to benefit the community and challenged ourselves to do so while overcoming color barriers that are ever present in this field. We hope this tool can be of use, and serves as a stepping stone to more robust ASL translation technology.
